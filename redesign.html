Network Redesign: Facility Accessibility

Performed a temporal accessibility analysis to assess travel times to major destinations, including a central hospital, from all other zones in the network using GTFS, OSM data and r5py library. The hourly travel time maps over a 19-hour period illustrates variations in public transport connectivity.

Network Redesign: High Temporal Resolution Reachability

Calculated 5-minute interval reachability from transit terminal during peak hours to provide a high-res understanding of accessibility dynamics. This approach ensures more accurate and meaningful insights for real-world transit system users by addressing temporal fluctuations in transit connectivity.

Network Redesign: Stop Accessibility

Conducted multiple stop-level accessibility analysis using actual walking times based on a speed of 4.8 km/h. For up to 8 minutes of walking, combined the resulting polygons with zonal boundaries to estimate the population's accessibility to stops within the defined walking time.

Network Redesign: Network Configurations

Two network configurations are compared: one prioritizing maximum ridership with reduced wait times and lower population accessibility (50%); the other focusing on maximum coverage, with increased population accessibility (75%) but with longer wait times, using the same level of resources.
